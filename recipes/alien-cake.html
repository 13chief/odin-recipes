Ingredients


  • 1 cup of stardust (make sure it's freshly harvested from the cosmos)
  • 2 cups of laughter (giggles from real aliens work best)
  • 3 UFO-sized eggs (found at the nearest intergalactic market)
  • 4 tablespoons of moonbeams (carefully collected during a full moon)
  • 5 cups of cosmic flour (you can get this at any celestial bakery)
  • A pinch of star spice (for that otherworldly kick)

Instructions


  1. Preheat your spaceship's oven to 375 degrees Kelvin (equivalent to 102.33 degrees Celsius on Earth).
  2. In a cosmic mixing bowl, combine the stardust and laughter until the mixture becomes a sparkling wonder.
  3. Whisk in the UFO-sized eggs one at a time, making sure each one beams with excitement.
  4. Gradually stir in the moonbeams, allowing the batter to glow with lunar delight.
  5. Slowly add the cosmic flour while dancing around the kitchen to your favorite alien tunes.
  6. Once everything is well-mixed, add a pinch of star spice for an interstellar flavor punch.
  7. Pour the batter into a round cake pan that's been lined with intergalactic parchment paper.
  8. Bake the cake in your spaceship's oven for approximately 30 minutes, or until a friendly alien probe comes out clean when inserted into the center.
  9. Let the cake cool for a few minutes, then transfer it to a flying saucer for serving.
  10. Enjoy the otherworldly deliciousness of your Alien Cake, and don't be surprised if it mysteriously disappears in a flash of light!
